Accomplishments During Presidency

  • 1845

    Annexation of Texas

    Texas officially joins the United States, escalating tensions with Mexico. Polk firmly supports expansionism, fulfilling his campaign promise of Manifest Destiny. This move heightens diplomatic friction, as Mexico views the annexation as an act of aggression. Polk’s bold stance sets the stage for future conflict.

  • 1846

    Oregon Treaty Signed

    The U.S. and Britain settle the Oregon boundary dispute, securing the Pacific Northwest along the 49th parallel. Polk had initially pushed for the entire Oregon Territory but compromised to avoid war with Britain. That same year, tensions with Mexico boil over, leading to the start of the Mexican-American War. Polk's expansionist policies continue to reshape U.S. borders.

  • 1847

    Mexican-American War Advances

    U.S. forces, led by generals like Winfield Scott and Zachary Taylor, capture Mexico City after a series of decisive battles. The war, though controversial, boosts Polk’s territorial ambitions. Back home, the conflict sparks debates over the expansion of slavery into newly acquired lands. Polk remains steadfast in his goal to secure vast western territories for the United States.

  • 1848

    Treaty of Guadalupe Hidalgo

    The war ends with this treaty, adding California, Arizona, New Mexico, and other territories to the U.S. — a massive land acquisition. This expansion fulfills much of Polk’s Manifest Destiny vision. However, the new territories intensify the national debate over slavery, foreshadowing future sectional tensions. Polk celebrates his victories, though his aggressive tactics have divided public opinion.
