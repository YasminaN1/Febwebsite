Retirement Plans

James K. Polk honored his pledge to serve only one term and did not seek re-election in 1848. He looked forward to a restful retirement in his newly purchased Nashville home, Polk Place.

Health Decline

The stresses of the presidency severely affected Polk's health. He embarked on a tour of the Southern United States, during which he fell ill, possibly contracting cholera.

Untimely Death

Less than four months after leaving office, Polk died on June 15, 1849, at the age of 53, making his retirement the shortest of any U.S. president.

Legacy

At the time of his death, thousands of Americans were moving westward into territories acquired during his administration, underscoring his significant impact on the nation's expansion.
